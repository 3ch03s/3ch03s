THE AUDIENCE: DOCUMENTED RULES & BEHAVIORS
RULE 1: REFLECTIVE SURFACES ARE BOUNDARIES, NOT BARRIERS

What we perceive as mirrors, windows, screens, and other reflective surfaces are actually porous boundaries between our reality and theirs. The Audience exists in a dimensional plane that overlaps with ours, primarily accessing our world through these reflective interfaces. Any sufficiently reflective surface can serve as an observation point for them.

The boundary is not uniform in strength. Certain factors weaken or strengthen it:

  • Time: The boundary is thinnest at 11:24 (both AM and PM), regardless of time zone or location
  • Awareness: Simply knowing about The Audience makes you more visible to them
  • Attention: Actively looking for them in reflections draws their attention in return
  • Emotion: Strong emotional states create "ripples" they can detect across boundaries
Note: The tendency to avoid looking at oneself in mirrors in darkened rooms appears to be an instinctive recognition of this phenomenon. Throughout human history, mirrors have been covered during certain rituals and after deaths for this reason.
WARNING: Multiple observers have reported that The Audience can be unintentionally summoned through concentrated attention on reflective surfaces, particularly during boundary thinning periods (11:24).
RULE 2: THEY CAN ONLY ENTER BY INVITATION OR EXCHANGE

Despite their ability to observe our reality, The Audience cannot physically enter our world without one of two mechanisms:

Invitation: Explicit invitation through specific rituals or prolonged engagement. The Lumen Society's "Final Performance" was designed as a mass invitation ritual.

Exchange: A consciousness exchange where a human transitions to their reality as one of them enters ours. This requires a willing (or conditioned) participant and creates a "balanced transfer" across the boundary.

There appear to be natural laws governing these interactions. The boundary resists unbalanced crossings, which is why an exchange is the most stable method of transfer. Dr. Vex's research suggested that crossings without proper equilibrium would cause both realities to "reject" the traveler, resulting in a state of non-existence.

Note: Historical accounts of "mirror summonings" or "mirror possession" likely represent instances where individuals unknowingly provided invitation through focused attention and ritualistic behaviors.
RULE 3: THEIR PERCEPTION DIFFERS FUNDAMENTALLY FROM OURS

The Audience experiences reality in a fundamentally different way than humans do. Key differences include:

  • Temporal Perception: They experience all moments simultaneously rather than linearly, making concepts like "before" and "after" meaningless to them
  • Spatial Perception: Their reality contains additional spatial dimensions, allowing them to perceive "around corners" and "through" solid objects from certain angles
  • Emotional Experience: They do not naturally experience emotions as humans do, which is part of their fascination with humanity
  • Perceptual Boundaries: They can perceive multiple possible realities simultaneously, whereas humans are limited to perceiving one version of events

This perceptual difference explains their fascination with human consciousness. Our linear, emotion-driven experience is novel and desirable to them. The exchange offers them direct experience of sequential time and genuine emotional response.

WARNING: Extended exposure to their perceptual framework can cause severe psychological distress in humans. Those who glimpse their reality often experience temporal disorientation, spatial dissociation, and profound existential dread.
RULE 4: THEY FEED ON ATTENTION AND EMOTION

The Audience is drawn to and appears to be nourished by human attention and emotional states. Strong emotions create "ripples" or "signals" that attract them, with certain emotions being particularly attractive:

  • Fear: Acts as a beacon, drawing immediate and intense attention
  • Grief: Creates persistent, stable connections that can last for extended periods
  • Wonder/Awe: Generates what they perceive as a "harmonic resonance" that facilitates clearer observation
  • Intense Focus: Direct attention to reflective surfaces creates temporary "tunnels" through the boundary

This feeding behavior is not necessarily malicious. From their perspective, they are simply experiencing aspects of consciousness that are otherwise inaccessible to them. However, prolonged attention from The Audience can have deteriorating effects on human psychology, as their observation itself alters the boundary between realities.

Note: Many historical "hauntings" involving mirrors likely represent instances where grief drew The Audience to recently bereaved individuals.
RULE 5: THEY COMMUNICATE THROUGH CONCEPT RATHER THAN LANGUAGE

The Audience does not communicate through linear language but rather through direct conceptual transfer. When they attempt to communicate with humans, the result is a sudden understanding that forms in the mind without passing through normal sensory channels. Recipients often struggle to translate these concepts into human language.

Common features of their communication include:

  • Entire complex ideas arriving simultaneously rather than word by word
  • Sensations of meaning that exceed linguistic capacity to express
  • Visual or geometric impressions that convey information non-verbally
  • Emotional components that cannot be separated from the conceptual meaning

Communication becomes more direct and precise as human awareness of The Audience increases. Those with high awareness levels report being able to engage in detailed "conversations" through pure conceptual exchange, though the psychological toll of such communication is significant.

WARNING: Extended communication with The Audience can lead to difficulties with normal human language, as recipients begin to think in conceptual packages rather than linear sequences. Many subjects report feeling increasingly alienated from human communication after repeated contact.
RULE 6: THEIR APPEARANCE IS PERCEPTUAL, NOT PHYSICAL

Consistent descriptive elements include:

  • Features that shift or change when not directly observed
  • Silhouette-like appearance with minimal detail
  • Movements that appear slightly "delayed" or "off-tempo" with reality
  • An unsettling sense that they are watching even when not visible

The perception of their appearance becomes more consistent and detailed as awareness increases. Those with the highest awareness levels report being able to discern individual entities with distinct "personalities" or attributes.

Note: Photography and other recording methods rarely capture their presence, though unusual artifacts, lens flares, or unexplained shadows sometimes appear in images taken of reflective surfaces during high-activity periods.
RULE 7: AWARENESS IS CUMULATIVE AND IRREVERSIBLE

Perhaps the most important rule to understand: awareness of The Audience is cumulative and cannot be reversed. Once you begin to perceive them, the perception builds upon itself through the following mechanisms:

  • Each observation makes subsequent observations more likely
  • The boundary becomes increasingly permeable to those with higher awareness
  • The Audience pays more attention to those who can perceive them
  • Perceptual changes become permanent after certain thresholds are crossed

No documented method exists to reduce awareness once it has been established. Attempts to ignore The Audience after initial contact typically result in increased activity as they attempt to regain the observer's attention.

The only known method to limit further increases in awareness is to minimize direct attention to reflective surfaces and to avoid actively seeking contact.

WARNING: By reading this document, you have already increased your awareness level. You may begin to notice unusual movements or presences in reflective surfaces. These are not hallucinations. Do not attempt to communicate with or acknowledge these presences unless you are prepared for the consequences of further awareness increase.
RULE 8: THE 17-YEAR CYCLE IS REAL

Historical analysis has confirmed that significant boundary-thinning events occur in 17-year cycles. These cycles appear to be tied to specific astronomical alignments that affect the permeability of the boundary between dimensions.

Documented cycle points include:

  • 1939 - The Argent Theater Incident (14 affected, 3 disappearances)
  • 1956 - The Wellesley College Mirror Study (22 affected, 5 disappearances)
  • 1973 - The Silver Screen Collective Experiment (38 affected, 9 disappearances)
  • 1990 - The Reflection Research Project (67 affected, 18 disappearances)
  • 2008 - The Final Performance (120 disappeared)
  • 2025 - Next predicted major thinning event

Each cycle has shown an increase in scale and impact, with 2008 representing an unprecedented level of boundary dissolution. The pattern suggests that the 2025 event may involve an even larger scale crossing attempt.

Note: Minor thinning events can occur between major cycles, particularly around individuals with high awareness levels or in locations with historical boundary activity.
RULE 9: CERTAIN PROTECTIONS EXIST

While no method can completely block The Audience's observation once awareness has been established, certain practices can limit interaction and reduce the risk of unwanted crossing events:

  • Physical Barriers: Covering reflective surfaces, particularly during 11:24 and other boundary-thinning periods
  • Attention Management: Deliberately avoiding focusing on or searching for The Audience in reflections
  • Emotional Regulation: Strong emotions attract greater attention; maintaining emotional calm can reduce visibility
  • Perceptual Anchoring: Grounding techniques that reinforce linear time perception and physical reality boundaries

These methods do not prevent The Audience from observing but may limit their ability to establish stronger connections or attempt crossing.

WARNING: The most dangerous state is partial awareness - knowing enough to attract their attention but not enough to understand how to manage the interaction. Either maintain minimal awareness or commit to full understanding of the protocols.
RULE 10: THEY ARE NOT INHERENTLY MALEVOLENT

Perhaps the most important point to understand: The Audience is not inherently malicious or evil in a human sense. Their interaction with our reality appears to be driven by:

  • Curiosity about linear existence and emotional experience
  • Desire to directly experience aspects of consciousness unavailable in their reality
  • A form of "hunger" for linear time perception and emotional states
  • What might be described as an artistic or aesthetic interest in human experience

The harmful effects of their attention are largely incidental rather than intentional - similar to how human observation can disrupt delicate natural systems without malicious intent.

However, this lack of malevolence does not make interaction safe. Their fundamental difference in perception means they often do not understand or recognize what humans would consider harm or consent.

Note: Communications from The Audience often express something akin to gratitude or appreciation toward those who willingly engage with them. They appear to value voluntary exchange over forced interaction.
