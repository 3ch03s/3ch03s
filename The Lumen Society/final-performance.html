THE FINAL PERFORMANCE - EVENT RECORD

On November 11th, 2008, the Lumen Society conducted what they referred to as "The Final Performance" at an undisclosed theater in Los Angeles. Attendance was by invitation only, with 108 guests selected through a process described in recovered documents as "preliminary conditioning via test screenings."

According to multiple sources, what began as an experimental film screening rapidly descended into mass psychosis, with reports of shared hallucinations among audience members. At precisely 11:24 PM, security cameras throughout the venue experienced simultaneous failure, leaving a 27-minute gap in surveillance footage.

When systems came back online, the theater was empty. All 108 audience members, along with the 12 Lumen Society members in attendance, had vanished. Their personal belongings remained, including phones, wallets, and in some cases, articles of clothing. No bodies were ever recovered, and the case remains open in LAPD files as a mass disappearance with suspected cult activity.

Subsequent investigation of the projection booth revealed modified equipment and film reels containing spliced frames with unusual geometric patterns. Analysis of these frames was never completed, as the evidence was reportedly destroyed in a fire at the police evidence locker three weeks later.

EVENTS LEADING TO THE FINAL PERFORMANCE

MARCH 2001

The Lumen Society is formed by Dr. Alistair Vex, a former experimental psychologist with alleged ties to MK-Ultra-adjacent research. Initial membership consists of seven Hollywood industry professionals, including directors, actors, and production staff.

Society manifesto speaks of "transcending the illusion of cinema" and "breaking the fourth wall between performer and audience in ways never before attempted."

JUNE 2003

First documented experiment with "24F" technique - the insertion of subliminal frames into film at precise intervals. Initial tests show promising results in altering viewer perception and emotional states.

Society membership grows to 19 individuals, with regular meetings held at a private residence in the Hollywood Hills.

OCTOBER 2005

Production begins on "The Mirror's Edge," an independent film serving as a cover for Project Lightway research. Cast and crew are selectively chosen based on psychological profiles indicating high susceptibility to suggestion.

First reports of crew members experiencing vivid shared hallucinations during filming. Dr. Vex's notes refer to these as "breakthrough moments" and "early contact with the Audience."

FEBRUARY 2007

Construction of the Mirror Chamber facility at an undisclosed location. Society records indicate this space was designed for "direct communion with the Audience" through specialized reflective surfaces and audiovisual stimuli.

First documented disappearance associated with the Society: camera operator David Mercer vanishes after reviewing test footage. His journal, recovered from his apartment, contains increasingly paranoid entries about seeing figures watching him from reflective surfaces.

APRIL 2008

Dr. Vex announces to Society members that "contact has been achieved" and begins planning for what he terms "The Final Performance." Attendance is described as mandatory for all members.

Society begins distributing modified short films to select audiences, ostensibly as "experimental cinema" screenings. These are later identified as conditioning tools, preparing viewers for the main event.

SEPTEMBER 2008

Series of unexplained events at Society headquarters: power fluctuations, equipment malfunctions, and reports of "shadow figures" captured on security footage.

Dr. Vex's final documented communication expresses excitement about "the thinning boundary" and confirms November 11th as "the perfect date when the stars align."

NOVEMBER 11, 2008

11:24 PM: All electronic systems in the building fail simultaneously. When power returns 27 minutes later, all 120 people in the theater have vanished without trace.

VENUE INFORMATION

The location of the Final Performance has been officially redacted from police reports, but multiple sources indicate it took place at the now-demolished Argent Theater on Sunset Boulevard.

The building was constructed in 1928 and had a history of unusual incidents, including a 1939 mass hysteria event during a film screening where 14 patrons reportedly experienced identical hallucinations.

Following the 2008 incident, the theater remained closed and was eventually demolished in 2010. The lot remains vacant, with multiple development projects abandoned after reporting issues with unexplained equipment failures and construction workers refusing to return to the site.
